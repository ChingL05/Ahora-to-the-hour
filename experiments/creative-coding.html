Design exploration · for review

Five quiet ways the page could keep time with you.

Each is generative, slow, and tied to the hour — extending the cover's "rain on still water" rather than inventing a new language. Nothing demands attention; each rewards staying. Move your cursor, or simply wait.

01

Still Water

The cover's raindrop, made into a place. The surface lies still until you disturb it; rings spread and meet and fade. Left alone, a slow rain begins to fall on its own — one drop every several seconds, the way the cover already behaves on return.

Tint drifts with the hour, the same curve the live site uses.

Would live as: the cover background, behind "Ahora"
the gradient is set to the real hour — it drifts all day
02

The Light Holds

"Stay as long as the light holds," made literal. A field of warm light that is dawn at dawn and dusk at dusk — computed from the visitor's clock and drifting continuously, so the page is never the same colour twice in a day. A breath of film grain keeps it from looking digital.

No interaction. It is weather, not a toy.

Would live as: a wash behind the whole page, or the invitation
a shaft of afternoon light · cursor moves the air
03

Dust, Late Afternoon

Motes adrift in a slanting beam — the particular light of a quiet room in the afternoon. They follow the faintest currents and stir, just barely, where your cursor passes, like a hand moving through still air.

A nod to the writing the album already keeps company with.

Would live as: behind the invitation, or section transitions
breathe with it — in as it opens, out as it settles
04

Breath

"Pause and reflect," offered as an instrument rather than a slogan. A ring that expands and eases closed at a calm pace — about five and a half breaths a minute, the rhythm that settles the body. The word at its centre is simply now.

The most literal reading of "to the hour": a single held minute.

Would live as: a quiet interstitial between album and blogs
dwell anywhere · the ink blooms and the paper drinks it back
05

Ink on Paper

Where you rest your cursor, ink blooms into the paper and slowly dries — sumi settling into a sheet. Nothing is permanent; the page always returns to white, so the reader can never spoil it.

The texture of the book section, made responsive.

Would live as: the paper field behind the blog pages

All five respect prefers-reduced-motion (they hold a still frame), pause when off-screen, and cost almost nothing when idle. My recommendation: Still Water on the cover is the surest win — it deepens what's already there. The Light Holds is the most "Ahora" idea of the set and the lowest-risk to ship sitewide. Tell me which one or two to take further and I'll build them into the real page.
